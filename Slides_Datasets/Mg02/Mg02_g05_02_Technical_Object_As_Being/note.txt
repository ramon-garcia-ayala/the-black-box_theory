TECHNICAL OBJECT AS CULTURAL BEING

Simondon's foundational claim:
technical objects are not neutral instruments.

They have a mode of existence.
They evolve. They relate. They develop.

A machine is not a fixed thing.
It is a process —
internally organized,
externally embedded,
historically situated.

This is why understanding a technical object
is not just engineering.
It is a cultural act.

And this is why refusing to understand it
— treating it as magic, or as mere servant —
is also a cultural failure.
