In the case of AI, data centers, and computational infrastructure 
are often kept far from view, almost invisible to the people who use them every day.

This is where Simondon identifies two opposite yet 
closely related ways of avoiding a genuine understanding of technical objects.

We either ignore them completely, treating them as black boxes whose inner workings do not concern us.

Or we elevate them to something almost mythical, attributing to them powers they do not actually possess.

In both cases, the result is the same: we fail to understand the machine itself. 

We either dismiss it or worship it, but rarely do we engage with it
